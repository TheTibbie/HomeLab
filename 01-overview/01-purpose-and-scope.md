# Purpose and Scope

## Purpose

This homelab is a continuously evolving environment built to demonstrate real infrastructure practice — not just the ability to get services running, but to own them: plan the design, document the reasoning, maintain operational hygiene, and improve over time.

Specifically, it covers:

- **Platform ownership** — virtualization design, capacity decisions, and service standardization
- **Operational maturity** — backups, retention policy, integrity validation, and monitoring
- **Networking fundamentals** — DNS filtering today; VLAN segmentation and edge firewalling next
- **Documentation discipline** — decisions recorded with context, not just outcomes

---

## Scope

This repo documents:

- High-level architecture and the reasoning behind major decisions
- Service inventory and placement — what runs where, and why
- Operational posture — backup approach, monitoring coverage, and recovery thinking
- Roadmap and planned improvements

---

## What this repo is not

This is not a production hardening guide, and it's not a step-by-step tutorial intended for copy/paste deployment. The documentation is written for an audience that can read between the lines — the goal is to show how decisions get made, not to hand-hold through every command.
